Cryptography

→

Cryptography is the art of writing information in a way that it's only readable to those who should see it and conceal it from eavesdroppers.

→

Applications of cryptography

↓   ↓   ↓

EARLY AGE

The early applications of cryptography were during the war times to convey secret messages.

↓

100 BC

One of the most mentioned cipher is the subsititution cipher, or most commonly known as Caesar cipher. Caesar used this to communicate to his army generals.

↓

MODERN AGE

Even in the modern age, cryptography were often as a tool of war.

↓

WORLD WAR II

Enigma was an enciphering machine used by the germans to encrypt their transmissions. Alan Turing, and his team played a key role in inventing a machine known as The Bombe. This machine was used to assist the code-breakers from deciphering german transmissions, and helped shorten WW2 .

↓

POSTMODERNITY

With the ubiquity of internet devices, the postmodern applications of cryptography are often seen in Cybersecurity.

↓

Messaging

Instant messaging technologies like WhatsApp uses end-to-end encryption to safeguard the user's messages from eavesdroppers.

↓

Transactions

Most of transactions today are done online. Websites protect critical information like credit card numbers or bank account numbers via Secure Sockets Layer (SSL).

CAESAR CIPHER

Caesar Cipher is named after Julius Caesar, a Roman military general.


It works by shifting the letters to the right for N number of times.

In Julius' case, he shifts 3 times.
